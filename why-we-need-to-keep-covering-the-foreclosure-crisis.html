Why We Need to Keep Covering the Foreclosure Crisis

Sun 05 July 2015 | tags: Foreclosure, -- (permalink)

Anna Clark wrote a great piece in the Columbia Journalism Review arguing for the need to continue coverage of the foreclosure crisis, as well as the value of collaboration and knowledge-sharing in the process. She mentions Christine MacDonald and Joel Kurth's work on foreclosures in Detroit and my collaboration with them.

Some seven years after the housing bubble burst, the story is still unfolding in many American cities: damaged neighborhoods, broken tax systems, and blighted homes; a search for justice and an accounting of blame; uneven progress toward recovery.

The experience of MacDonald and Kurth shows the value of drawing on expertise from outside the boundaries of your newsroom.
