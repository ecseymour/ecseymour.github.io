The Downtown That Never Was

Wed 02 December 2015 | tags: talks, -- (permalink)

Interesting talk by Taubman alum Conrad Kickert in which he challenges the idea that there was a time of mid-century maximum downtown density from which Detroit has since fallen. According to Kickert, Detroit's downtown was always already being dismantled and remade into empty — or auto-filled — surfaces.
